Part A · The customer story

Open Customer app → tap Log in twice to reach the Home screen, then walk these scenes.

1
Onboarding & the closet
From the chooser: Customer app → Sign up to show the flow (style, sizes, body shape, pronouns, avatar, consent), then Log in → Home → My closet.
In the closet, tap the Shoes / Dresses category chips and type in search — the grid filters live. Open an item to edit its details, favourite, archive, or delete.
Proves: photo-to-closet capture, AI item detection, and a real wardrobe the user can manage over time — the foundation the whole product sits on.
2
The core: AI outfit matching
Home → Match an outfit → choose from one item or whole closet → pick an occasion → Generate outfit.
On the result: Swap a piece, tap Why this works, use the Cheaper/More formal refine chips, and note the missing item row links to shopping.
Proves: the headline feature — closet-aware matching, on-avatar preview, explainable AI, and the affiliate shopping bridge for gaps.
3
Save, compare & wear
Save the look → Saved. Filter collections with the chips. Open Compare looks and change the Look A / Look B dropdowns — the table, costs and buttons rebuild live.
On any outfit tap Wear today → it logs to Home → History, and item wear-counts build up over months.
Proves: the product gets more useful the longer it's used — saved looks, side-by-side decisions, and a real wear history.
4
Social & feedback loop
Outfit → Share → send to an in-app friend. Then Profile → Social to see Shared with me, give feedback (Wear it/Not this one + comments), and add friends.
Proves: the complete two-way sharing loop — send a look, get opinions back, and grow a friends network.
5
Monetization — the business model
Note the ad banner on Home (free plan). Go to credits pill → Plans → Subscribe to Plus → pay. Watch the ads disappear and the plan flip to Plus.
Then Manage subscription: cancel, auto-renew, the payment-failed banner → update card, and invoices under payment history.
Proves: all three revenue streams — credits, subscription (ad-free), and affiliate — plus the real billing lifecycle a long-term user hits.

Part B · The admin panel

Back to the chooser (Log out) → Admin panel → Continue → Verify and log in → Dashboard. On mobile, the ☰ menu opens navigation.

6
Operations
Users (search, suspend), Brands → open a partner → pause/remove, Products & Taxonomy management.
Proves: the team can run the catalogue and user base — every control resolves to a real action.
7
Revenue & AI
Monetization & Ads toggles, AI tuning, Reports/moderation, and Support tickets (filter by status, reply, resolve).
Proves: the levers behind the customer app — pricing, ads, AI behaviour and support — are all operable.

Notes for the conversation

Why greyscale? This is a high-fidelity structural prototype, not a visual design. Presenting it in greyscale keeps the client focused on flow, completeness and logic; colour and branding come in the design phase.
Scope discipline. The prototype maps to 98% of in-scope proposal requirements (see the Coverage link up top). A few ideas were deliberately left out as future considerations — closet sort/bulk-select, a visible "your taste is learning" profile, and notifications mark-all-read — so scope stays tight for v1.
It's genuinely interactive. Filters filter, dropdowns recompute, upgrading removes ads, logging a wear updates history. Verified: 0 broken buttons, 0 runtime errors, clean on mobile and desktop, customer and admin.
